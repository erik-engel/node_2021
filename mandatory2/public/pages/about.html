Motivation


My passion for Computer Science


My general interest in computers have always been a part of me, from when I was young and started playing games on a computer, like many other young ones. I have always wondered just how computers worked and it was kind of a mystery to me as a kid. It seemed like magic to me, that you could simply click some buttons and something great happend in front of you. So learning about the mystery of computers and even how to program your own software is very exciting to me.


I have had a great drive towards many goals in my life. When I started at KEA I could really feel that I had chosen the right education. In my first semester I thought that I would never learn to code because of the steep learning curve. But once I had changed my mindset towards more logical and abstract thinking, things took off and everything started to click into place, and learning to program seemed way easier for me to comprehend.


I recently landed a job as a tutor on my school, where I help my fellow students, mainly the first and second semester students. I love to help others, where I can. And helping people in computer science is especially satisfying, as you get to help other people get that eureka-kind-of-moment, where everything comes together. I have also helped students from my own class in my spare time, and I enjoy that aswell. For me helping others, is at least as beneficial for me, as the student I am helping. As I get to think more about certain methods and datatypes, I normally would not work with as much.


As a person, I am very detail oriented and determined. I do not stop working on a problem before it is solved. And when I put my mind to something, I have to do it all the way. This comes into play, when i code. I like to code and learn for many hours at a time, because "Just 10 min of coding" quickly turns into me reading about something new, that I did not knew i needed to know about, before i sat down and started typing. If i work with problems I know little or nothing about, I don't quit. I will find a way to solve the problem, even if I have to use tools, I have never used before. With google and stackoverflow, you can come a long way on your own.
